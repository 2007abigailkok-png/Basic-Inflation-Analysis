## Insights:

1) Cellphones had by far the most volatile index from 2008 to 2026, with a standard deviation of 252.7020, followed by three other Technological devices - Televisions, Laptops and Tablets (with standard deviations of 90.5592, 72.1782 and 43.8246 respectively). This reflects high price volatility in technology goods, likely driven by rapid product cycles, changes in demand, and shifting input costs.

2) In keeping with the findings in (1), the Information and Communication Division had the most volatile index from 2008 to 2026, with a standard deviation of 132.8695. This was significantly higher than other divisions' standard deviations (for example, Recreation, sport and culture, the second-most volatile division with a standard deviation of 29.7493 of its index).

3) The product which experienced the most growth in its index from 2008 to 2026 was Electricity (717.1778% growth). This is significantly above growth of the Water Supply index(the product index which experienced the second-most growth at 462.8141%). This aligns with widely reported increases in electricity tariffs in South Africa over the period, rising substantially due to infrastructure strain, rising input costs, and capacity constraints.

4) The item index which experienced the largest month-by-month decimal change is fresh tomatoes, of which the index increased 47.8589% from September to October 2012. This spike may reflect seasonal supply constraints and short-term volatility in fresh produce pricing.
